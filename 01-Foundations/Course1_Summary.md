## Key Cybersecurity Concepts Learned
- Understanding the CIA Triad and how confidentiality, integrity, and availability guide security decisions
- Differentiating between threats, vulnerabilities, and risks
- Recognizing common attack vectors used by threat actors

## Role of a SOC Analyst
- Monitoring security alerts and system logs
- Identifying suspicious activity and potential security incidents
- Following documented incident response procedures
- Escalating confirmed threats to senior analysts

## Security Controls Overview
- Preventive controls to stop attacks before they occur
- Detective controls to identify suspicious behavior
- Corrective controls to restore systems after an incident

## Common Threats and Indicators
- Phishing attempts and social engineering indicators
- Malware and ransomware behavior
- Brute force login attempts and account compromise
- Unauthorized access attempts

## Why This Foundation Is Important
This foundational knowledge establishes how cybersecurity teams protect organizations, prioritize threats, and respond to incidents. It prepares me to contribute effectively in an entry-level SOC role by understanding both the technical and operational aspects of security monitoring.
